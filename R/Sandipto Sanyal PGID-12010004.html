Question 3 ¶

Since the experiment is of binary outcome True or False and also has more than 1 trials = 15 it is a case of binomial distribution with n = no. of outcomes = 15, probability of success of each outcome = p = 0.5

(a) What is the probability that the student got first five questions correct.¶

This means we have to find P(C,C,C,C,C,W,W,W,W,W,W,W,W,W,W). For each question probability of success is 0.5. So for five questions it is 0.55 and for rest of the 10 questions probabilit of failure will be (1-0.5)10.
Since we are wanting probability that the student got first 5 question correct at a row we will calculate the required probability as 0.55 x (1-0.5)10

In [10]:
probabilities = (0.5)**5*(0.5)**10
paste(c('probability that the student got first five questions correct ', probabilities), collapse = " ")
'probability that the student got first five questions correct 3.0517578125e-05'

(b) What is the probability that the student got five questions correct¶

For this we want to know the probability that the student got any of the 5 question correct. Obviously this probability is expected to be much higher than what we found in previous question.

  1. Firstly we are choosing any five questions out of 15 questions. So no. of choices will be 15C5.
  2. Now for each question probability of success is 0.5. So for five questions it is 0.55 and for rest of the 10 questions probabilit of failure will be (1-0.5)10.
  3. Thus total probability of our student getting any 5 questions correct is 15C5 x 0.55 x (1-0.5)10
In [11]:
nCr = dim(combn(15,5))[2]
probabilities = nCr*0.5^5*0.5^10
paste(c('probability that the student got five questions correct ', probabilities), collapse = " ")
'probability that the student got five questions correct 0.091644287109375'

  1. We know in a normal distribution 68% of the data lies between +/-1 standard deviation.
  2. So mean - 1sd = 35 and mean + 1sd = 42.

  4. Standard deviation = 42 - mean = 3.5

Question 7 ¶

Given we have coin toss means we have two ouputs and more than one trials we are dealing with a random variable which follows binomial distribution. The following data is given:

  1. no. of trials = n = 3
  2. Probability of success i.e. having Head = p = 0.5

(a) probability of getting all three heads¶

We are asked to find P(HHH) = 0.5^3 = 0.125

(b) probability of getting exactly 1 head¶

For this

  1. We first have 3C1 choices for getting only 1 head in all 3 trials.
  2. Probability of getting 1 head is 0.5
  3. Probability of not getting other tosses as head is (1-0.5)2

So the required probabality = 3C1 x 0.51 x (1-0.5)2 = 0.375

Question 9 ¶

Here we have two sets having color mix of M&Ms as follows:

  • 1994: (30% Brown, 20% Yellow, 20% Red, 10% Green, 10% Orange, 10% Tan)
  • 1995 and afterwards: (24% Blue, 20% Green, 16% Orange, 14% Yellow, 13% Red, 13% Brown)

So we have to find the probability of P(Bag from 1994|M&M is yellow).
From total probability theorem P(M&M is yellow) = P(M&M is yellow|Bag from 1994) x P(Bag from 1994) + P(M&M is yellow|Bag Not from 1994) x P(Bag Not from 1994) = 0.2 0.5 + 0.14 0.5 = 0.17

By Bayes theorem we know that P(Bag from 1994|M&M is yellow) = P(M&M is yellow|Bag from 1994).P(Bag from 1994)/P(M&M is yellow) = (0.2 * 0.5)/0.17 = 0.588
